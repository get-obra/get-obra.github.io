**A virtual employee for the businesses software forgot:** the ones run by one person wearing every hat. Obra quietly handles the operational work (guest messages, document processing, compliance filings, scheduling, accounting prep), brings you only what genuinely needs a human, and never acts without your yes.

It is not a chatbot and not another tool you have to drive. It is a worker that logs into the systems you already use, does the multi step jobs a person would do, and reports back when it is done. You keep the decisions. It does the work.

## Why Obra is different

Putting AI to work in a real business is not held back by capability. It is held back by trust. The honest objection is "I am not letting software touch my guests, my filings, or my money unless I can see exactly what it did and stop it." Obra is built so that trust is structural, not a promise you are asked to take on faith.

- **Operator-blind.** We never see your data. Not because we promise it, but because the system is built so that we cannot.
- **Local-first.** It runs on your own machine, under your own Claude key. No cloud holding your information, no central database, no data sharing.
- **Two reads must agree.** Anything critical, like a passport number or an invoice total, is read twice and independently. The two reads have to agree before it moves. If they disagree, it stops and hands it to you.
- **An audit you control.** Every action is signed and replayable, and the record is yours to export. The proof belongs to you, not to us.
- **Your yes, always.** Nothing that touches the outside world happens without your approval. Obra prepares the work and waits for your tap.

Together these are the difference between "an AI that might do something" and "a worker you can actually hand the keys to, because you can see and stop everything it does."

## How it works

Obra is powered by Claude, and you bring your own key, so the intelligence is yours and so is the bill. It runs where you want it: a small server, your own machine, your own infrastructure. Claude is the power. Obra is the plug that lets a real business receive that power safely.

A typical job looks like this: a trigger arrives (a booking email, a document, a scheduled time), Obra does the steps a member of staff would do, anything critical is double read and checked, and anything that affects a guest, a regulator, or your accounts is held for your approval before it goes out. When it is done, you get a clear report and a record you can keep.

## The first vertical: hospitality

The first place Obra works is short term rental hosting in Portugal and across the EU, where a single host is quietly responsible for guest registration, identity checks, and compliance filings that the booking platforms never finish for them. The gap between what the platforms hand over and what the law requires is real, and today it is closed by hand, late at night, one guest at a time.

Read the thesis: [The Trust Gap](https://get-obra.com/the-trust-gap). It explains why this gap exists at the channel layer, and why only a local-first design can close it without becoming the very data risk it set out to remove.

The brand is sector neutral by design. Hospitality is the wedge, not the ceiling. The same engine carries over to any operational, regulated, small business, one vertical at a time as the library grows.
