The problem

AI can't be trusted with regulated work.

Frontier models are powerful enough to run real operations. A model can read a passport, draft a filing, answer a customer. It can also invent a detail with total confidence, and you would not know until a regulator did. Confidence is not correctness.

That is why the businesses that most need an AI employee, the ones buried in compliant, deadline-driven, audit-heavy work, are exactly the ones that cannot safely adopt one. Their data cannot leave the building. Every action needs a receipt. One wrong move costs more than the automation saves. The blocker is not the model. It is everything around the model.

The solution

Obra is the harness around the model.

Obra wraps a frontier model in everything it needs to be trusted with real work: a connector framework so it can act inside real systems, a workflow runtime that makes multi-step jobs repeatable, a trust-enforcement layer that governs every action, and an audit trail the customer owns. The model supplies the intelligence. The harness supplies the trust, the control, and the proof.

How it works

Trust, by construction.

Most AI agents can't be trusted with regulated work. Obra can, because trust is built into the architecture, not promised in a policy. Six properties, each one enforced by the system itself.

Local-first, your key
Obra runs on your own machine under your own Claude key. Raw data never leaves your control.
Two reads must agree
Critical extractions are read by two independent Claude passes. They must agree before the work moves. If they don't, it stops and comes to you.
Three-gate trust
Risky actions pass three gates: structured reasoning, an adversarial second read by a separate Claude, then your approval.
Typed actions
Every action is typed Autonomous, Gated, or Forbidden. The model cannot bypass a tier. Forbidden stays forbidden.
Audit you own
Every action is hash-chained to the one before it. Tamper-evident, and yours to export the moment a regulator or accountant asks.
Operator-blind
We structurally cannot see your data or your actions. Not a promise, a property of how the system is built.
