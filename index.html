The reality

Every booking starts a clock.

Inês rents a couple of rooms on the coast. The law gives her 24 hours from a guest's arrival to verify their passport and report it to the police, so she needs to collect it before they ever reach the door, to check it in person and keep the arrival smooth.

But the passport is the hardest thing to ask for. The guest booked in good spirits, and then a stranger they have never met asks them to hand over their passport. Some hesitate, some grow suspicious, and some cancel the booking outright. Her messages, run through a translator, come out stiff and cold, which only makes the ask easier to distrust. Hospitality runs on no friction, and the law drops friction into the one moment that decides whether a guest stays at all. Every booking, the same gamble.

What Obra does

Then she has Obra.

When a booking arrives, Obra goes to work. She writes the guest a warm first message in their own language, the kind that makes the passport feel safe to hand over instead of strange to be asked. She reads the details, prepares the report for the police, lines up the cleaning, gets the month ready for the accountant. The quiet, repetitive parts simply move.

And the moment something matters, the report to the regulator, anything touching a guest or Inês's name, Obra stops and waits for her. A tap from her phone is enough, even when she is far from a desk. The message goes out in her voice, so the guest only ever feels they were welcomed by her. The deadline is met, and every decision that mattered stayed hers.

A real Saturday, coordinated start to finish. This plays on its own.
She does the work. The host keeps the decisions. And the guest only ever feels they were welcomed by her.
How Obra is built

Trust, by construction.

Here is why you can hand Obra the things that frighten you. She runs on your own machine, under your own Claude key, so your data never reaches us. Anything that carries weight passes three gates: Obra reasons it through, a second pass challenges it, and you give the final yes. Every step is written into an audit trail only you hold, the kind a regulator, an auditor, or a future you can check line by line.

Operator-blind
We never see your data, and not because we promise it. The system is built so we cannot. You hold the Claude key, not us.
Reads must agree
Two independent reads of anything critical have to agree before it moves. If they disagree, it stops and comes to you.
Audit you control
Every action signed and replayable, yours to export the moment a regulator, an accountant, or your own doubt asks.
Your yes, always
Nothing that touches the world happens without your approval. The action your business takes is the one you signed.
